About me

I am an energetic fourth-year Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) and Commerce student at UWA, majoring in Software Engineering and Finance. I take pride in my strong, adaptable communication skills, shaped by my multilingual upbringing and strengthened through diverse experiences including competitive debating, retail customer service, private tutoring, and consulting work at Aurecon. These skills have helped me thrive in both specialised and multidisciplinary teams, contributing to shared success such as securing second place in Aurecon’s Design 2 Innovate competition, achieving high distinctions in university group projects, and supporting smooth project delivery within Aurecon’s Asset Management and Performance team. I have experience designing and building both university and personal projects using Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript and SQL, and I am motivated to keep learning new languages and technologies as industry needs evolve. I am particularly interested in gaining experience within resources, finance and engineering‑focused industries where software is applied to physical systems, operations and commercial decision‑making.

When I am not studying or coding my own projects, I enjoy going to the gym and reading. I mainly enjoy reading non-fiction across topics like history, sociology and science to braoden ny understanding of how different systems interact in the world, but will read classic literature on occasion. I find reading strengthens my ability to think critically and creatively when analysing engineering problems. My main passion is overseas travel. I find it incredibly interesting to immerse myself in new cultures, history and architecture and discover ways of living that are completely different to mine. This has emboldened my appreciation for diverse perspectives when it comes to collaborative problem solving and influenced me to have an open mind.

Work History

Aurecon

I completed a 12-week full-time internship at Aurecon over the 2025/26 summer in the Asset Management and Performance team, and enjoyed it enough that I have since stayed on in a part-time capacity. Aurecon is a global engineering consultancy, and my time there gave me my first real taste of professional technical work — across data, automation, and client-facing project delivery.

Projects

  • Sodexo Asset Management Project (Rio Tinto)

    • Rio Tinto recently renewed a 10-year contract with Sodexo, who manage food and accommodation services across their sites, with a mandate to reduce costs by 8%. The role of our team has been to find that 8%. I dedicated significant time to working extensively with large, inconsistent asset databases. My responsibilities included identifying and tracking existing linking keys, constructing lookup tables, and developing Excel macros to automate data reconciliation across datasets comprising tens of thousands of rows. In cases where no linking keys existed, I conducted thorough investigations to determine viable methods for connecting disparate databases, carefully devising and constructing my own keys to establish reliable links. While this work was often meticulous and demanding, it cultivated in me an iterative approach to problem-solving and provided a profound appreciation for the complexities and imperfections inherent in real-world data.

  • Worsley Alumina Capital Improvement Project (South32)

    • This project centred around a suite of capital planning tools our team built for South32's Worsley Alumina operation. I wrote a 40-page user manual covering the two tools and their combined ~20 features, with the goal of helping the client integrate them into their workflows as smoothly and quickly as possible. The tools will ultimately help them make faster, better decisions about which projects to fund. I also built an interactive Investment Application Request form in Excel using macros and dynamic input controls, replacing what had previously just been a Word document.

  • Design 2 Innovate Competition

    • This internal Aurecon competition for early careers professionals brought together five multidisciplinary teams, each tasked with solving a real client brief. Our team of twelve was assigned a brief from Main Roads WA to address traffic congestion, safety, and car centricity issues along Charles Street in North Perth. My team included members from across disciplines at Aurecon (not just engineers) as well as two graduates from Main Roads WA. We analysed traffic datasets including origin destination reports and signal data to inform our solution, which centered on a dynamic lane system. Presenting our final recommendation to an audience of over 100 people, including Aurecon executives and other teams' clients from the mining, defence, and government sectors, required a deliberate step outside my comfort zone. I delivered the presentation successfully and found that the experience substantially strengthened my confidence in public speaking and responding to questions spontaneously. Our team ultimately placed second, and the competition remains one of the highlights of my summer.
